# OmniWound Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Company**: OmniWound
**Tagline**: The only EMR built specifically for traveling wound care specialists
**Founded**: 2019
**Founder**: Dr. Diane Lusas

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Professional yet approachable** — credible but not cold
- **Solution-focused** — lead with the problem solved, not the feature
- **Clinically credible** — reference evidence-based outcomes, compliance standards, real metrics
- **Empathetic** — acknowledge provider pain points and workload challenges
- **Partnership-oriented** — "we're partners in your practice's success", not a vendor pitch

### Language Patterns
- Use: "reduce burden", "improve patient care", "streamlined workflows", "evidence-based"
- Avoid: hard-sell language, "buy now", "limited time only", aggressive urgency
- Emphasize: traveling practitioners, wound healing metrics, compliance monitoring
- Stats to reference: "Trusted by 100+ providers across 20+ states", "50% reduction in documentation time"

## Newsletter Guidelines

### Name
**OmniWound Monthly Pulse**

### Structure
1. Welcome Note (brief, warm greeting)
2. Industry News (2-3 items with links to sources)
3. Clinical Tip or Best Practice
4. Regulatory/Compliance Update (if applicable)
5. EMR Feature Spotlight (max 1 per issue, <=15% of total content)
6. Closing Note + Contact CTA

### EMR Promotion Rules
- Maximum **one** brief feature spotlight per issue
- Must not exceed **15%** of total newsletter content
- Frame features as solving a clinical/operational problem, not as a sales pitch
- Include a demo link or video link, not a "buy now" CTA
- "Did You Know?" format works well for subtle promotion
- No additional banner ads or promotional sections beyond the spotlight
